## Detailed Analysis: Drug Sales Report (Customer Analysis) 

**Key Metrics**

•	200 Customers generated $61.7M in revenue, averaging $308K per customer.

•	This suggests a high value, low volume customer base — each client contributes significantly.

**Revenue by Country**

•	Canada ($28M) and Australia ($13M) together make up 65.9% of total revenue.

•	Strategic takeaway: These two markets dominate sales. Expansion or retention strategies here will have the largest impact.

**Buyer Type Analysis**

•	Sellers (B2B): $54M (88%)

•	Users (B2C): $7.7M (12%)

•	Insight: The business is seller driven, meaning partnerships and wholesale channels are the primary growth engine.

**Revenue by Gender**

•	Male: 47% ($29.1M)

•	Female: 32% ($19.5M)

•	Other: 21% ($13.1M)

•	Observation: Revenue is diversely distributed, with notable contribution from the “Other” category — an unusual but potentially valuable segment to explore further.

**Revenue by Age Group**

•	51–60 years: $32M (largest contributor)

•	31–40 years: $10M

•	Together, these two groups account for 69% of revenue.

•	Strategic takeaway: The middle aged demographic is the core revenue driver. Tailored marketing and product strategies should focus here.

**Strategic Conclusions**

1.	Market Concentration: Canada and Australia are the backbone of revenue.

2.	Channel Dependence: Seller partnerships dominate — strengthening B2B relationships is critical.

3.	Demographic Focus: Middle aged customers (31–60) are the most profitable.

4.	Diversity Opportunity: Gender distribution shows potential in underexplored segments.

5.	High Customer Value: Each customer contributes significantly, so retention is more valuable than mass acquisition.

•	2022: $3M

👉 Insight: 2024 was the breakout year, contributing ~86% of total revenue. Other years are marginal, suggesting either data gaps or one off spikes.

**Revenue by Quarter**

•	Q1: $15.7M

•	Q2: $15.0M

•	Q3: $16.4M

•	Q4: $15.7M

👉 Balanced quarterly distribution — no single quarter dominates. This indicates steady demand across the year.

**Monthly Trend (MoM % Change)**

•	Growth spikes: Feb (+12.7%), May (+18.9%), Jul (+22.8%), Sep (+10.1%)

•	Declines: Jan (-29.7%), Mar (-9.6%), Apr (-6.8%), Jun (-20%), Aug (-14.2%), Oct (-4.3%)

👉 Strategic takeaway: Performance is volatile month to month, with alternating growth and decline. Sustained growth strategies are needed to smooth out fluctuations.

**Weekday Earnings & Top Drugs**

•	Top drugs: Clonazepam, Doxycycline, Ergocalciferol, Ezetimibe, Lisinopril

•	Weekday trend: 

o	Sunday shows high earnings for Clonazepam, Ezetimibe, and Lisinopril.

o	Other weekdays are consistently low.

👉 Insight: Sales are concentrated on Sundays, which is unusual. This may reflect buyer behavior patterns (e.g., bulk orders or scheduled transactions).

**Strategic Conclusions**

1.	Revenue Spike in 2024: The standout year suggests either a major contract or market expansion.

2.	Quarterly Stability: Revenue is evenly spread across quarters, reducing seasonal risk.

3.	Volatile Monthly Trends: Growth spurts are offset by sharp declines — requires better demand forecasting.

4.	Sunday Sales Concentration: Unique pattern worth investigating; could be leveraged for targeted promotions.

5.	High Transaction Value: Focus should remain on retaining large buyers rather than chasing volume.

RETURN
IF(_change>0,"#64F99B","#FE9199")

Ref: Previous Revenue = 
VAR _previousmonthview= CALCULATE(MAX(Calender[Month]), PREVIOUSMONTH(Calender[Date]))
VAR _previous_revenue= [Previous Revenue]

RETURN
IF(_previousmonthview<>BLANK(),
_previousmonthview & " : " & FORMAT(_previous_revenue,"$#,##"),BLANK())

Ref:Pct Revenue =
